Mental Health Assessments

Disclaimer: These assessments are for educational purposes only and are not a substitute for professional diagnosis or treatment. If you're experiencing mental health difficulties, please consult with a healthcare professional.
Anxiety Assessment (GAD-7)

The GAD-7 is a validated self-report questionnaire used to assess generalized anxiety disorder symptoms.

Time to complete: 2-3 minutes

About the test: 7 questions assessing frequency of anxiety symptoms over the past two weeks.

Over the last 2 weeks, how often have you been bothered by the following problems?

1. Feeling nervous, anxious, or on edge

2. Not being able to stop or control worrying

3. Worrying too much about different things

4. Trouble relaxing

5. Being so restless that it's hard to sit still

6. Becoming easily annoyed or irritable

7. Feeling afraid as if something awful might happen

Depression Assessment (PHQ-9)

The PHQ-9 is a validated self-report questionnaire for depression screening, diagnosis, and monitoring.

Time to complete: 3-5 minutes

About the test: 9 questions based on the DSM-IV criteria for depression.

Over the last 2 weeks, how often have you been bothered by any of the following problems?

1. Little interest or pleasure in doing things

2. Feeling down, depressed, or hopeless

3. Trouble falling or staying asleep, or sleeping too much

4. Feeling tired or having little energy

5. Poor appetite or overeating

6. Feeling bad about yourself - or that you are a failure or have let yourself or your family down

7. Trouble concentrating on things, such as reading the newspaper or watching television

8. Moving or speaking so slowly that other people could have noticed. Or the opposite - being so fidgety or restless that you have been moving around a lot more than usual

9. Thoughts that you would be better off dead, or of hurting yourself in some way

Important: If you're having thoughts of harming yourself, please seek help immediately:
  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 1-800-273-8255
  • Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741
  • Or go to your nearest emergency room
Stress Assessment (PSS-10)

The Perceived Stress Scale (PSS-10) is one of the most widely used psychological instruments for measuring the perception of stress.

Time to complete: 3-5 minutes

About the test: 10 questions about feelings and thoughts during the last month.

The questions in this scale ask you about your feelings and thoughts during the last month. In each case, please indicate how often you felt or thought a certain way.

1. In the last month, how often have you been upset because of something that happened unexpectedly?

2. In the last month, how often have you felt that you were unable to control the important things in your life?

3. In the last month, how often have you felt nervous and stressed?

4. In the last month, how often have you felt confident about your ability to handle your personal problems?

5. In the last month, how often have you felt that things were going your way?

6. In the last month, how often have you found that you could not cope with all the things that you had to do?

7. In the last month, how often have you been able to control irritations in your life?

8. In the last month, how often have you felt that you were on top of things?

9. In the last month, how often have you been angered because of things that were outside of your control?

10. In the last month, how often have you felt difficulties were piling up so high that you could not overcome them?

About Mental Health Assessments

Mental health assessments are tools that help identify symptoms of common mental health conditions. They can provide insights into your mental wellbeing and help you understand when it might be appropriate to seek professional support.

Benefits of Regular Assessment:
  • Early detection of potential mental health concerns
  • Increased self-awareness of your emotional state
  • Tracking changes in your mental health over time
  • Identifying when professional support might be beneficial
Important: These assessments are not diagnostic tools and should not replace consultation with qualified healthcare professionals. If you're experiencing significant distress or receiving concerning results, please reach out to a mental health professional.
